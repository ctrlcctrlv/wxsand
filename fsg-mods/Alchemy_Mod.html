Recipe Book

Tips: It's always best to add water onto the ingredient, as most ingredients float on water, making mixing difficult. If a potion needs to be heated, it is advised to heat the mortar from below with a burner, and not a Heatplate, unless the recipe says otherwise. It is advised to wait a short while before feeding another potion to your subject

[edit]

Digitalis

Type: Poison

Effect: Cardiac arrest upon ingestion. Fatal.

Preparation: Simply add water and Foxglove together.

[edit]

Firebrew

Type: Potion

Effect: Causes spontaneous combustion upon ingestion. Not fatal.

Preparation: Mix Firesalts and water together.

Facts: It is advised to mix this potion in a heat-proof container, as the final product gives off heat.Firebrew is an inefficient cure for the effects of Frostbrew, is raises the subject's temperature to normal but doesn't remove the ice. However, the ice can be melted off (see below). Attempts to consume raw Firesalts result in the salts bursting into flame, which can cause damage to equipment and the subject.

[edit]

Frostbrew

Type: Potion

Effect: Causes massive drop in temperature and causes a film of ice to spread across the surface of the subject. Not fatal.

Preparation: Mix Frostsalts and water together.

Facts: Attempts to consume raw Frostsalts result in the mouth of the subject being coated in ice. This ice can sometimes spread across the body, but can be melted off with Heatplate, fire, or turning up the temperature in the room. Caution is advised when using fire to remove the ice. Frostbrew is an extremely inefficient cure for the effects of Firebrew. Do not expect any real results.

[edit]

Mugwort Tea

Type: Potion

Effect: Cures various strange conditions upon consumption. Preparation: Pour water over some mugwort leaves, then heat to 50º, once the entire mixture is pale green, the tea is ready.

Facts: Cures the effects of Firebrew and Frostbrew. Tastes terrible, however.

[edit]

Invisibility Potion

Type: Potion

Effect: Renders the drinker completely invisible.

Preparation: Fill the bottom of a container with water. Cover the top of the container with a filter and place Aloe Vera onto the filter. Once it is all filtered, remove the filter and add redwort to the solution. Once the mixture turns purple, the potion is ready.

Facts: It is strongly recommended that you leave the container used for this potion in place, as you will need it to locate the subject to administer the antidote.

[edit]

Invisibility Antidote

Type: Antidote

Effect: Returns the drinker to visibility after drinking an invisibility potion.

Preparation: Filter some Aloe Vera into a container and add Mugwort. The solution shuold turn a pale blue.

Facts: This antidote can be administered anywhere, in case you loose track of where the subject's mouth is.

[edit]

Vomiting Potion

Type: Poison

Effect: Causes constant vomiting shortly after consumption.

Preparation: Add some sugar to water, and then sprinkle some Ipecacuanha into the mix. Once the mixture turns dark brown, it is ready.

Facts: The vomitting, while it does not pose and threat to your subject, will not stop until cured by alchemical methods. It also makes a terrible mess...

[edit]

Peppermint Tea

Type: Potion

Effect: Cures nausea and vomitting.

Preparation: Simply add peppermint to water.

Facts: Would be very useful if your subject were to... 'accidentally' ingest some Vomitting Potion..

Exploding Potion

Type: Poison

Effect: Depending on spices used in the creation of this poison, it causes minor to huge explosions within the subject.

Facts: When mixing a Magnitude 5 or 6 Exploding Potion, it is advised that you do so in a Container, as a simple mortar cannot withstand the heat and corrosiveness given off by these potions. Once you've mixed the required spices you can pour the mix from a Container onto a Heatplate. Giving raw Blair's Reserve to a subject will result in loss of mouth and surrounding tissue. There have been talk amongst alchemists that one unlucky alchemist mixed Firebrew with a Magnetude 6 Exploding Potion and gave it to his subject. He and the surrounding city were never seen again.

Discoverer: Kurisu Sand

Erasing Potion

Type: Poison

Effect: A very gory death. More information in facts section.

Facts: Drinking this potion is a VERY bad idea. It is absorbed into your bloodstream the moment you drink it. You now have anything from 1 to 30 seconds to live. When it goes off, you'll feel a wave of pain travel through your body, but it gets worse when it reaches your skin. You skin will immediatley flake off and give way to torrents of blood, and eventually your entire body does the same. Extremely painful and not a nice way to go.

Preparation: Mix up some Digitalis, then add sunflower oil. The mixture should turn grey. When it does, add sugar, which should turn it a paleish red. Then you need to put it through a filter, which will turn it a brighter red. Finally, add jsut a tiny pinch of firesalts to finish it. This should cause the potion to start rapidly changing colours. When it does this, it is ready for ingestion.
